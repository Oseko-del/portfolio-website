Creating a Landing Page that Performs Better

A landing page is often the first thing a potential client or employer sees. It has one job — to convert a visitor into an action. Whether that action is sending a message, booking a call, or clicking a link, everything on the page should push toward that goal.

Design Comes First

Before writing a single line of code, I think about the visual hierarchy. What should the visitor see first? What should they feel? The hero section needs to communicate who you are and what you do in under 5 seconds.

Performance Matters

A beautiful page that loads slowly loses visitors. I keep things fast by using plain HTML/CSS/JS instead of heavy frameworks, compressing images, and avoiding unnecessary dependencies.

Key Performance Tips

Use system fonts or load only what you need — Google Fonts can slow down your page if you load too many weights.

Compress all images — Use WebP format where possible. A 2MB hero image is a page killer.

Lazy load below-the-fold content — Don't load what the user hasn't scrolled to yet.

Clear Calls to Action

Every section should guide the visitor toward one clear next step. Avoid multiple competing buttons. One strong CTA button with a contrasting color converts better than five weak ones.

The combination of clean design, fast loading, and a clear CTA is what separates a portfolio that gets responses from one that gets ignored. These are the principles I apply to every project I build.
